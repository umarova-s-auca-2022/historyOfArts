Welcome to the History of Art course!

During the first week (January 12--18), we studied and discussed the selected works of art across time devoted to the Greek myth of the abduction of  Europa. The images of these works are given below.  You were asked to choose two of them to compare and discuss how they interpreted the Greek myth. You submitted your interpretation in the forum below.

This week (January 19--25), we begin our study of the art of Ancient Greece. First of all, it is important to gain a general understanding of the Ancient Greek Culture. 

For this purpose, first, read the following sections from  Laurie Adams' Art across Time, vol. 1 : Cultural Identity, Government and Philosophy (pages 135-140); Greek gods and Roman counterparts (p. 140); Literature and Drama and "Man is the Measure of all things" (p.141) 

Secondly, we will discuss the topic of Pottery paintings in Ancient Greece. Read section 'Painting and pottery' (p. 141-149) in Adams' Art across Time,  vol. 1. 

The images of the pottery paintings are included below.

Another topic is Greek sculpture of the Archaic and Classical styles. The reading is pages 150--158 in Laurie Adams' Art across Time, vol.1. The pictures for the sculpture to be discussed are uploaded here too.

The readings are also available as a PDF file: The Art of Ancient Greece, posted below.  This is a scanned copy of  Lauri Adams' Art across Time, vol. 1.
