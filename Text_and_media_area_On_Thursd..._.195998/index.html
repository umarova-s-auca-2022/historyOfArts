Description

The guidelines for the test (mid-term).

The topics for the test are the art of Ancient Greece, the art of the Renaissance including Italian Renaissance, the art of  Mannerism, Baroque, Rococo, and Neoclassicism, as based on our readings.

Ancient Greek art

Archaic: from Geometrical and Orientalizing to black figure pottery painting (Exekias)

Sculpture: New York Kouros and Peplos Kore

Classical: red figure painting (Berlin painter, Penthesilea painter, Niobid painter) and white ground painting

Sculpture: Kritios Boy, Miron’s Discobolus, Polykleitos’ Spear Bearer and Wounded Amazon

Late Classical sculpture: Lysippos’ Aproxyomenos and Hercules, Praxiteles’ Aphrodite of Knidos

Hellenistic period. Sculpture: Boxer, Laocoon and his two sons, head of Alexander, Winged Nike of Samothrace, Dying Gaul

Greek Temples: the Temple of Zeus at Olympia with metope sculpture relief of Heracles and Atlas and pediment relief of Apollo with Lapith and Centaur, Phidias sculpture of Zeus

The Parthenon and the statue of Athena by Phidias

 Renaissance

Early Renaissance: Donatello, Botticelli, Masaccio, Montegna

High Renaissance: Leonardo, Michelangelo, Rafael

Venetian School: Giorgione, Titian, Tintoretto

Mannerism: El Greco

Baroque: Bernini; Caravaggio; Artemisia Gentileschi; Rubens; Velasquez, Rembrandt, Poussin

Rococo: Jean-Honore Fragonard, Francois Boucher

Neoclassicism: Jacques-Louis David

The test will ask you to analyze and compare the works of individual artists of these periods and explain the subject-matter (the content) of their works (what is it that they represent/show in their works?), the style of their works (How do they represent the subject-matter/content?), and how their works represent the general characteristics of their artistic period (for example why can this Greek sculpture belong to Archaic period or to Hellenistic period? Or what are the characteristics of this painting that allow us to attribute it to Mannerism?).

For this, you are expected to have an idea about the common characteristics of style of these artistic periods and artistic devices/methods used to create artistic works, the changes that occur in the artistic periods over time, know the major artists and their major works (the artists and works included in the e-course) and be able to tell the differences between works of different artistic periods. Finally, you are expected to have some idea how the art on these periods was influenced by historical developments and how it reflected the culture of the covered periods.

For example, you may be asked to identify the artist, his work, attribute his work to a concrete artistic period and explain what artistic traits of this work help us to refer him/her to this artistic period and the subject matter of the work.
