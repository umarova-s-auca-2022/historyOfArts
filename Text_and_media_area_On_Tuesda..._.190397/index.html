Description

On Monday, Feb 9, we will conclude the consideration of the Early Renaissance in Italy by considering  Mantegna,  Donatello, and more of Botticelli, and then we'll discuss the  High Renaissance masters, Leonardo. and early Michelangelo. 

Readings in Adams'  Art across Time: p. 546,  brief info about the High Renaissance in Italy, and the insert Julius II: the humanist Pope;  Leonardo da Vinci, pages 552-556; sfumato, p. 555; and the early sculptures of Michelangelo,  pages 556-558. 

Adam's book Art across Time contains useful inserts in the text to supplement information on these techniques, so read the inserts on linear perspective (box 'line of vision' p. 490), on atmospheric perspective (read the box 'a distant haze' p 500), sfumato (box p 500), oil painting (box p. 516). Also cast a look at boxes 'Julius II: Humanist Pope' p 550 and 'The Platonic academy' p 528 that shed some light on the art patrons of the time.

Watch the YouTube presentation of Sotsby's of Leonardo's painting Salvatore Mundi. What is your reaction to this sale?  Write in the forum.

On Wednesday, Feb. 11, we will consider Michelangelo, pages 558-866, and Rafael, pages 567-572
