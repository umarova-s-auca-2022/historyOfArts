Description

On Monday, Feb. 2, we will conclude the discussion of the art of Ancient Greece by considering the Greek temples. 

Please read Chapter 6 from Elliot's book about Ancient Greece, which discusses the downfall of the Greek Empire  and answer the following question in the forum: What does the chapter believe the causes of the Greek downfall are?

On Wednesday, February 4, we begin a new topic: Renaissance. We will start wth the discussion of the Early Renaissance. From now on,  we will use Volume 2 of Adam's book.  The PDF file of the chapter from Adams' Volume 2 is also uploaded here.

Read from Adams’ Art across Time (note that it is the second volume of her work—Greek art is in the first volume), ch. 14 The Early Renaissance, the following material:  Renaissance Humanism p.p. 481-484,  artists Masaccio (p. 496), Donatello (p. 504-505 and 529), Andrea Mantegna (p. 523), Botticelli (p. 527). Also read inserts Humanism (p. 481)., Lines of Vision (p. 490-492), Distant Haze (p.500), Oil Painting (p. 516).  We will not take Fifteenth-Century Painting in Flanders, which begins on p.531.  

 Additional reading (not mandatory): To understand the socio-historical background of the Renaissance, its newness and connection to Greek art, you are recommended to read Renaissance: the foundation of Western Art p. 451-457; p. 480-481 in Gardner’s Art through Ages . 

We will start the topic with the activity based on viewing the two-part video The Masters of Illusion (the file with the video is uploaded in this section).
